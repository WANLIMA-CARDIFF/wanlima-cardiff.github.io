About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the University of Cambridge, specialising in the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and satellite remote sensing. In my current role, I focus on AI-enabled disaster damage assessment (such as blasts, floods, and fires) as part of the EPSRC-funded MicroBlast project. Prior to joining Cambridge, I completed my PhD in Computer Science at Cardiff University in 2024, working within the Visual Computing Group under the supervision of Dr. Oktay Karakus (Remote Sensing) and Prof. Paul Rosin (Computer Vision). I received my Master's Degree in image and video communication and signal processing with Distinction from the University of Bristol in 2021.

My research focuses on developing advanced computer vision techniques, with a particular emphasis on semi-supervised learning, active learning, and multi-modal fusion. I am dedicated to bridging the gap between theoretical AI and its practical application in satellite remote sensing imagery. Specifically, my work focuses on providing vital solutions for urgent real-world problems, including marine monitoring, urban infrastructure detection, and rapid disaster damage assessment for floods, hurricanes, and explosions, etc. Beyond remote sensing, I maintain a strong interest in applying computer vision to robotics and industrial automation. In this space, I have successfully developed and commercialised automated facilities for the manufacturing sector.
